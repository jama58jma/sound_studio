1. INTRODUCTION

This Safety Protocol establishes the mandatory procedures and rules for the safe operation of all audio, recording, and monitoring equipment within the sound studio. Its purpose is to prevent injuries, protect personnel, and ensure the continuous and reliable operation of studio systems. All staff, students, and visitors must understand and comply with these safety requirements before entering or using the facility. Failure to follow these rules may result in equipment damage, personal harm, or disciplinary action.

The protocol applies to all areas of the studio, including the control room, live room, isolation booths, equipment storage spaces, and any associated technical workstations. Because audio studios involve electrical equipment, elevated sound levels, manual handling tasks, and environmental hazards, users must be aware of the risks and follow the preventive measures described in this document.

2. GENERAL RULES

  1. Follow all posted safety signs and instructions. They must not be ignored or removed.
  2. Maintain clear pathways. Cables, cases, stands, and personal items must not obstruct walkways.
  3. Operate equipment only if you are trained and authorized. Untrained users must not power on or configure technical systems.
  4. Food and beverages must not be placed near any electronic equipment. Spills can cause electrical shock, short circuits, or fire.
  5. Maintain professional conduct. Running, rough behavior, or unnecessary noise must not occur inside the studio.
  6. Use proper lighting. Users should ensure that workspace lights are adequate to avoid tripping hazards or misread controls.
  7. Report all hazards immediately. Damaged cables, overheating devices, or unsafe conditions must be reported to the supervisor.
  8. Keep the studio clean and organized. Dust buildup, clutter, and trash should be removed promptly.
  9. Use correct lifting techniques. Heavy speakers, racks, or instrument cases must be lifted with assistance if needed.
  10. The studio door must always remain closed during recording sessions to prevent noise interference and maintain acoustic safety.

3. SPECIFIC PROCEDURES

A. BEFORE USING EQUIPMENT

  1. Conduct a visual inspection.
    • Check all power cables, connectors, and devices for signs of damage or wear.
    • Ensure that cables are coiled or routed safely and do not create trip hazards.
  2. Verify electrical safety.
    • Ensure that power strips are not overloaded.
    • Confirm that all equipment is connected to grounded outlets or power conditioners.
    • Equipment must not be plugged in or unplugged with wet hands.
  3. Set up ergonomically.
    • Adjust chairs, desks, and monitor heights to maintain neutral posture.
    • Position speakers and screens at appropriate angles to reduce strain.
  4. Check environmental conditions.
    • Ventilation systems should be active to prevent heat accumulation.
    • Ensure that no flammable materials are placed near warm equipment.
  5. Prepare your workspace.
    • Remove drinks, unnecessary personal items, or loose objects.
    • Ensure that all required PPE is available before powering any system.
  6. Review session requirements.
    • Confirm input/output routing, file storage location, and backup procedures.
    • Communicate session plans clearly to all participants.

B. DURING USE

  1. Follow proper start-up procedures.
    • Power on equipment in the correct order (typically outboard gear → interfaces → monitors → DAW systems).
    • Users must not bypass safety switches or protective covers.
  2. Monitor sound levels.
    • Sound pressure levels should remain within safe listening thresholds.
    • The operator must use limiters, volume controls, and metering tools to avoid sudden peaks.
    • Personnel must leave the room if exposed to high sound levels for extended periods.
  3. Use safe cable management.
    • Route cables along walls or cable ducts.
    • Loose cables must not cross main walking paths.
  4. Protect equipment from overheating.
    • Ensure adequate airflow around amplifiers and processors.
    • Equipment must not be covered during operation.
  5. Handle equipment carefully.
    • Microphones, stands, and instruments must be adjusted slowly and securely.
    • Heavy equipment must not be moved without assistance.
  6. Maintain communication.
    • Inform others before changing monitor levels, patching signals, or initiating recordings.
    • Headphone users should be warned before sending high-level audio signals.
  7. Avoid distractions.
    • Mobile phones should be silenced.
    • Unnecessary conversation must not occur in the control room during active sessions.

C. AFTER USING EQUIPMENT

  1. Follow proper shutdown procedure.
    • Reduce volume levels to zero before powering off monitors.
    • Turn off equipment in reverse order (monitors → interfaces → outboard gear).
  2. Disconnect equipment safely.
    • Cables must be unplugged by gripping the connector, not the cable itself.
    • Power cables must not be pulled or twisted.
  3. Store equipment correctly.
    • Coil cables using approved over-under method.
    • Return microphones, stands, and accessories to designated storage areas.
    • Ensure cases are closed and shelves are organized.
  4. Clean the workspace.
    • Wipe surfaces with approved cleaning products.
    • Remove trash, tape, or debris.
    • Report any damaged or malfunctioning equipment.
  5. Secure the studio.
    • Ensure that doors are closed, lights are off, and all systems are fully powered down.
    • Lock the studio if required by facility policy.

4. EMERGENCY PROCEDURES

A. ELECTRICAL EMERGENCIES

  • Disconnect power immediately using the main studio switch or circuit breaker.
  • The affected device must not be touched if smoke, sparks, or burning smell is present.
  • Notify a supervisor and contact emergency services if necessary.
  • Do not attempt repairs unless qualified and authorized.

B. FIRE EMERGENCIES

  • Activate the fire alarm and evacuate the building using designated exit routes.
  • Use only approved fire extinguishers (CO₂ or dry powder) for electrical fires.
  • Do not use water-based extinguishers on electronic equipment.
  • All personnel must assemble at the designated meeting point.

C. MEDICAL EMERGENCIES

  • Call emergency services immediately.
  • Provide first aid only if trained.
  • Do not move injured individuals unless they are in immediate danger.
  • Report the incident to the studio supervisor.

D. ACOUSTIC EMERGENCIES

  • If sudden loud noise or feedback occurs, reduce monitor levels immediately or mute the main output.
  • Headphone users must remove headphones to prevent auditory injury.
  • Investigate the cause only after levels are safely reduced.

5. PERSONAL PROTECTIVE EQUIPMENT (PPE) REQUIREMENTS

Hearing Protection

  • Earplugs or earmuffs must be worn when exposed to high sound levels or during equipment testing.
  • Personnel should always have hearing protection available in the studio.

Fire Extinguisher

  • Fire extinguishers must be clearly visible and easily accessible in all studio areas.
  • All personnel must know the location of the nearest fire extinguisher and be familiar with the PASS method (Pull, Aim, Squeeze, Sweep) for use.
  • Use the extinguisher only for small, contained fires. In case of a major fire, follow the Evacuation Procedures immediately.

Emergency Exit

  • Emergency Exits must be clearly marked with the appropriate green signage and remain completely unobstructed at all times.
  • All personnel must be familiar with the evacuation routes and the designated meeting point outside the facility.
  • Do not use elevators during a fire or emergency evacuation.

No Smoking

  • Smoking is strictly prohibited within all areas of the sound studio and the facility.
  • This prohibition includes traditional cigarettes, e-cigarettes, and vaping devices.
  • Smoking poses a fire risk and can damage sensitive studio equipment.

Electrical Hazard

  • Always inspect cables, power strips, and connectors before use to ensure they are not damaged, frayed, or exposed.
  • Equipment must be connected only to properly grounded outlets or power-conditioned systems.
  • Users must not handle plugs, switches, or electrical equipment with wet hands.
  • Liquids must be kept away from all electrical devices to prevent electrical shock, short circuits, or fire hazards.
  • If any equipment emits smoke, sparks, or unusual smells, it must be turned off immediately and reported to the supervisor.

No Drinks Near Electrical Equipment

  • Food and beverages must not be placed on or near audio consoles, computers, monitors, amplifiers, or any electronic devices.
  • Spilled liquids can cause severe damage, including electrical shock, short circuits, and permanent equipment failure.
  • All drinks brought into the studio must be stored at a safe distance from equipment, and preferably in closed containers.
  • Any spill must be cleaned immediately and reported to the supervisor.
